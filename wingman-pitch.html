The shift

Dating is becoming entertainment.

Love Island and Love Is Blind are cultural events. Dating creators pull millions of views filming real dates. Livestream tipping turned watching into a paid, interactive sport. Three waves are colliding, and nobody has built the thing in the middle: a live, interactive, always-on dating show.

📺

Reality dating

The most-watched format on streaming. People are obsessed with watching strangers fall for each other.

🎥

Dating creators

Creators film real dates for huge audiences, with no native way to monetize the moment.

🪙

Live tipping

Twitch and TikTok proved audiences will pay, live, to change what happens on screen.

Why it doesn't exist

It lives in a gap nobody sits in.

🎞

Hollywood can't

Reality TV thinks in seasons and broadcast and has no live interactive-platform DNA. It cannot run an always-on, audience-controlled product.

💻

Silicon Valley won't

Big tech can build the live platform, but will not touch "pay to shape real people's romance" because of the brand and regulatory liability.

Building this means being a production company and a tech platform and willingly owning the human responsibility that scares both sides off. Almost nobody sits in that intersection. The wall is not technology, it is willingness, and that is exactly the moat.

The insight

The crowd is the editor.

Reality TV is riveting because of editing. Producers carve 40 great minutes out of a thousand hours of raw footage. Take that away and live dating is mostly awkward silence. That is why every always-on streamed house has died of boredom.

Our answer: the audience does the editing, live. Plays, votes, and a pot on the line manufacture the moments that editing normally provides. The crowd is no longer watching the show. They are making it. That single mechanic is the bet, and it is the most original thing here.

Supply & the flywheel

Everyone is a matchmaker.

Casting authentic, magnetic people is the hard part, and money makes it worse, not better. So we turn the crowd into the casting engine: nominate a single friend, back a pairing, or put yourself forward. Watchers become matchmakers, matchmakers bring the contestants, and the transcripts plus viewership teach the AI who to cast next.

Business model

The house pays four ways.

Core

Wings virtual currency

Viewers buy Wings to send plays and votes. We take a fee on every transaction, the rest splits between contestants and the host.

Stakes

The nightly pot

A crowd-funded pool the most-loved contestant wins. It drives rooting, spending, and a reason to tune in for the finale every night.

Brands

Sponsored nights & gifts

A drinks brand sponsors the round-of-drinks play. A label sponsors the house. Native integrations, not banner ads.

Upside

Format & clip licensing

The house generates endless clippable IP, and a network of houses per city becomes a media franchise.

Guardrails

The show built to protect its people.

Audience-controlled rejection has harmed people before and triggered real duty-of-care regulation. We treat that as the core product, not compliance, and it is a brand no cheap copycat can match.

  • +Shift-based, never locked in. Contestants come for a night and go home. No always-on confinement.
  • +Opt-in, vetted, 18+. Screening before, on-call support during, real aftercare, and a tap-out anytime.
  • +Hard caps on the AI and the crowd. No optimizing for looks or humiliation, and the crowd can never override a contestant's hard no.
How we prove it

One night tells us everything.

The only real unknown is whether a live, audience-directed date is actually watchable. We answer it cheaply before building anything: one rented house, six contestants cast from our network, one dating creator driving the audience, plays plus a live outcome vote plus a small pot.

📊

Watch it for

Retention past 40 minutes, share of viewers who interact, and how many clippable moments per night.

✅

Go if

People stay, the crowd directs instead of lurking, and we get clips worth reposting.

🚫

Walk if

People drop off even with the controls, casting fills with exhibitionists, or it ever feels exploitative.

The vision

Start with one night that proves the format, then one house, then a network of always-on dating houses with recurring fan-favorite contestants. The audience does not just watch love happen. They cast it, direct it, fund it, and cannot look away.
